Redcurrant Seeds

Complete Guide for Growing Ribes rubrum

Seed Details

Collection Date: 10 July 2025

Source: Fresh fruit bought from Lidl, Austria

Storage Advice: Keep seeds in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Use within 1 year for best germination results.

Quick Facts

Scientific NameRibes rubrum
FamilyGrossulariaceae
Plant TypeDeciduous shrub
Fruit ColorBright red
TasteTart, slightly sweet
UsesJams, juices, desserts, garnishes

Planting Instructions

  1. Cold Stratification: Redcurrant seeds require a chilling period. Place seeds in moist sand inside a sealed bag and refrigerate for 3 months.
  2. Sowing: After stratification, sow seeds 0.5 cm deep in seed trays filled with well-draining compost.
  3. Germination: Keep soil moist and place trays in a bright, warm location (18–20°C). Germination may take several weeks.
  4. Transplanting: When seedlings are large enough to handle, transplant them outdoors in spring after the last frost.
  5. Spacing: Plant 1–1.5 meters apart to allow for full growth.

Care & Maintenance

  • Sunlight: Full sun to partial shade.
  • Watering: Keep soil consistently moist, especially during dry spells.
  • Fertilizing: Apply a balanced fertilizer in early spring.
  • Pruning: Prune in winter to remove old wood and encourage new growth.
  • Pest Control: Watch for aphids, sawflies, and fungal diseases. Use organic controls when possible.

Fun Fact

Redcurrants have been cultivated in Europe for centuries, and in medieval times, they were valued not only for their taste but also for their medicinal properties, often used to soothe fevers.
